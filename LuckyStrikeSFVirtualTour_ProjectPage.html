Lucky Strike San Francisco 2020:
Virtual Tour

A two part project consisting of:
1 - Multi-platform virtual tour of the San Francisco Lucky Strike Entertainment as a marketing proposal to show the venue off to potential clients

2 - A means of proposing renovation suggestions to refresh the physical location with a unique aesthetic and flow that suits what its surroundings are and will be beyond the year 2020.

With the exponential rise in travel times around the general San Francisco - Bay Area, the tour is designed to be a method of showing the venue to potential clients without them having to come to the physical location. A virtual tour created for web, mobile and VR displays would broaden the ability for clients to view the venue and what it has to offer in a fun as well as interesting way.

The restaurant and bowling have become well known and loved by its immediate neighbors. But like its home in the city by the bay, its once loved theme and feel have faded and are in need of rediscovery. Building a tour such as this comes with dual opportunities as the Lucky Strike Corporation can use the tour to also view suggestions on exploring what a new, refreshing feel might be, with a few suggestions already having been applied as this project has evolved.

Starting with building the venue to scale in a virtual space, which required measuring every detail of the physical location. From the walls, windows and doors… all the way down to the specific beer taps, as well as the salt and pepper shakers used.

Once the entire venue was mapped out down to an 1/8th of an inch and the basic structural models were built and placed, styling each piece to match a certain theme and to instigate a specific flow through the building came based on the features that had already been put into play.

What’s labeled as the Einstein Room is considered the venues ‘gaming lounge’. This is one of the rooms that are bound by the wall that’s responsible for initiating the project, and is the specific room that caused it to become a fully developed experience.

With a personal love and fascination in regards to the individual and mind that was Albert Einstein, the aesthetic design decision was to use the theme of genius and expand it to show several brilliant individuals deemed as geniuses from all around the world, including Einstein's first wife, Mileva.

By placing pieces of art that displays their extraordinary work strategically with the proper lighting and contrast, a flow of interesting visuals pulls viewers through the building that in its own right becomes a tribute to those that have quite literally changed the world through the power of imagination and creation. Some of the traits of San Francisco that have always made it such a special place, and characteristics about it that can never fade or be extinguished no matter the era.

VIEW PROJECT

It actually began as a simple modeling project to show how a particular wall in the venue might be done differently. Most people that have seen the wall agree that the wall paper used to decorate it is unpleasant to look at, and it really just doesn’t fit anywhere with the original theme. And so I was asked for some suggestions from the local management, them knowing I was a recent design graduate.

I used the project as a way of testing and honing some of my skills after grad school. Alot was learned during school of course, and I wanted to apply that knowledge to a few small projects to develop my personal methods on doing the things I enjoy and truly want to explore.

Soon though, the project evolved from changing a wall, to changing a room, to remodeling the entire venue and a complete semi-interactive experience in development.
